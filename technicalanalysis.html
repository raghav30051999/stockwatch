Technical Analysis

Technical Analysis is amethod of evaluating securities by analyzing statistical trends gathered from trading activity, such as price movement and volume.

These are some of the indicators used in technical analysis:

  1. Moving Average: The portion of a company's profit allocated to each outstanding share of common stock.A smoothed-out line that represents the average price of a security over a specified period, helping identify trends and support/resistance levels.
  2. Support and Resistance: Levels where a stock's price tends to find support (does not fall below) or encounter resistance (struggles to rise above) on a chart.
  3. Trend Lines: Lines drawn on a chart connecting two or more price points, used to identify the direction and strength of a trend.
  4. Relative Strength Index (RSI): A momentum oscillator that measures the speed and change of price movements, indicating whether a security is overbought or oversold.
  5. MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence): A trend-following momentum indicator that shows the relationship between two moving averages of a security's price.
  6. Volume: The number of shares or contracts traded in a security or market during a given period, indicating the level of interest or activity in the stock.
  7. Candlestick Patterns: Patterns formed by the open, high, low, and close prices of a security over a certain time period, used to predict future price movements.
  8. Breakout: A price movement through a predefined level of support or resistance, signaling a potential continuation of the current trend.
  9. Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD): A trend-following momentum indicator that shows the relationship between two moving averages of a security's price.
  10. Fibonacci Retracement: A technical analysis tool used to identify potential support and resistance levels based on the Fibonacci sequence of numbers.
  11. Bollinger Bands: Volatility bands plotted above and below a moving average, helping traders identify overbought and oversold conditions.

These are just some of the terms you'll encounter when learning about the technical analysis. It's important to continue learning and researching as you navigate the world of investing.

Basic Terminology used in Stock Market

For a beginner in the stock market, these are some of the key terms to be known for better understanding :

  1. Stock: Represents ownership in a company; owning shares means owning part of the company's assets and earnings.
  2. Market Capitalization (Market Cap): Total market value of a company's outstanding shares, calculated by multiplying share price by total shares outstanding.
  3. Shares: Units of ownership in a company; buying shares makes you a shareholder and gives you a portion of the company.
  4. Stock Exchange: Marketplace for buying and selling securities like stocks, bonds, and commodities; examples include NSE, BSE, NYSE and Nasdaq.
  5. Dividend: Payment made by a company to its shareholders from its profits, often in cash or additional shares.
  6. Portfolio: Collection of investments owned by an individual or entity, including stocks, bonds, mutual funds, and ETFs.
  7. Bull Market: Financial market with rising prices and positive investor sentiment, usually over an extended period.
  8. Index: Benchmark used to measure the performance of a group of stocks or securities, like S&P 500 or Dow Jones Industrial Average.
  9. Volatility: Measure of the variance of price movements of a security or market index over time; high volatility means big change in price/volume.
  10. Broker: Licensed professional or firm that executes buy and sell orders on behalf of investors, usually for a fee or commission.
  11. Market Order: Order to buy or sell a security at the current market price, executed immediately at the best available price.
  12. Limit Order: Order to buy or sell a security at a specified price or better, executed only if market price reaches the specified limit.
  13. Diversification: Risk management strategy spreading investments across different assets to reduce exposure to any single investment or risk.

These are just some of the fundamental terms you'll encounter when learning about the stock market. It's important to continue learning and researching as you navigate the world of investing.

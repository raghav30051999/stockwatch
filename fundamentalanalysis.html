Fundamental Analysis

Fundamental Analysis is a method of evaluating a stock by analyzing the fundamental factors that influence its intrinsic value, such as financial statements, management quality, industry trends, and economic indicators.

These are some of the financial statements/indicators used in Fundamental Analysis :

  1. Earnings Per Share (EPS): The portion of a company's profit allocated to each outstanding share of common stock.
  2. Price-to-Earnings Ratio (P/E Ratio): A valuation ratio calculated by dividing the current market price of a stock by its EPS.
  3. Price-to-Book Ratio (P/B Ratio): A valuation ratio comparing a company's market value to its book value, calculated by dividing the current market price per share by the book value per share.
  4. Dividend Yield: A financial ratio representing the dividend income per share relative to its price, expressed as a percentage.
  5. Return on Equity (ROE): A measure of profitability calculated by dividing net income by shareholders' equity.
  6. Debt-to-Equity Ratio: A financial ratio indicating the relative proportion of shareholder equity and debt used to finance a company's assets.
  7. Profit Margin: A measure of a company's profitability, calculated by dividing net income by revenue.
  8. Revenue Growth: The percentage increase in a company's revenue over a specific period, often compared year-over-year.
  9. Cash Flow: The net amount of cash and cash equivalents moving into and out of a business.
  10. Free Cash Flow: The cash generated by a company's operations after accounting for capital expenditures.

These are just some of the fundamental terms you'll encounter when learning about the stock market. It's important to continue learning and researching as you navigate the world of investing.
